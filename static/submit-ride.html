Submit Your Own Ride

Help map Richmond's bike lane hazards by contributing your own recordings.

What You Need

Every submission requires two files recorded during the same ride:

🎥 A video file MP4, MOV, or similar. Camera mounted to your bike, pointed forward down the lane.
📍 A GPX file GPS track exported from your phone or bike computer. Contains your route and timestamps.
Timing is important. The video and GPS tracker should be started at roughly the same moment. The system matches each video frame to a GPS coordinate using timestamps — if they're out of sync, detections will be pinned to the wrong location on the map.

Step by Step

  1. Mount your camera. Attach your phone or action camera to your handlebars or helmet, aimed forward along the bike lane. Make sure the lens is clear and the recording will capture the road surface ahead.
  2. Open your GPS tracker app and have it ready to start — but don't start it yet. See the apps section below for options.
  3. Start both at the same time. Hit record on your camera and start your GPS tracker as close to simultaneously as you can. Even a few seconds off is fine — major drift over the course of a ride is the thing to avoid.
  4. Ride your route. No special technique needed. Just ride normally through the bike lanes you want to document.
  5. Stop both recordings when you're done and save them.
  6. Export your GPX file from the tracker app (instructions below) and transfer both files — video and GPX — to wherever you'll upload from.
  7. Upload using the form below. Submit both files together and the system will handle the rest.

GPS Tracker Apps & GPX Export

Any app that records a GPS track and can export a .gpx file will work. Here are a few options:

GPX Tracker Recommended
  1. Open the app and tap Start to begin recording.
  2. When your ride is done, tap Stop and save the track.
  3. Go to your saved tracks, select the ride, and tap the share/export icon.
  4. Choose Export as GPX and save or share the file to your device.
Strava GPX export — not yet tested
  1. Record your ride as normal in Strava.
  2. After saving, open the activity on the website (not the app).
  3. Click the ⋯ (more) menu → Export GPX.
  4. Note: Strava's free tier may limit export options.
Ride with GPS GPX export — not yet tested
  1. Record your ride in the app.
  2. Open the activity on the website.
  3. Click Export → GPX File.
  4. A basic free account should support GPX export.
Not sure which app to use? GPX Tracker (iOS/Android) is the simplest option — it's purpose-built for exporting GPX files and requires no account. Strava and Ride with GPS are great if you already use them for tracking rides, but the GPX export step requires logging in on their website rather than the app.

File Requirements

Video: MP4, MOV, M4V, AVI, or MPEG. Any resolution is fine, though higher quality will produce more accurate detections. Try to keep the camera stable.

GPX: Standard .gpx format. Must contain timestamped trackpoints (most apps export these by default). Route files without timestamps won't work.
